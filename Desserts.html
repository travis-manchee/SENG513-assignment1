Maple-Bourbon Banana Cake

This easy dessert from Food & Wine results in a banana-bread-like texture with a rich maple bourbon sauce to moisten it. Serves great with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream, as they tones down the sweetness of the sauce.

Servings: 6-8 Servings

Ingredients

  • 6 tablespoons salted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup berry sugar
  • 1 overripe banana, mashed
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup whole milk (room temperature)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 3/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons bourbon
  • 1/4 cup pecans, finely chopped

Directions

  1. Pre-heat oven to 375F (190C). In a deep 2-quart ceramic baking dish, add melted butter.
  2. Whisk in berry sugar and mashed banana, mixing until thoroughly combined. Whisk egg and milk into mixture.
  3. In a separate bowl, whish flour, baking powder and salt. Whisk mixture into baking dish until loose batter is formed.
  4. In a microwave-safe cup, heat maple syrup, light brown sugar and 1/2 cup of hot water until hot (~1 min).
  5. Add bourbon to syrup cup, stirring well to combine. Drizzle mixture over batter in crosshatch pattern; it will seep to bottom. Do not stir. Add pecans on top of batter.
  6. Set baking dish on rimmed baking(to avoid spill over) and bake for 30 minutes, until cake is golden. Do not open oven during baking.
  7. Let cool for 5-10 mins. Serve in bowl with ice cream or whipped cream.
